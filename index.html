What is sustainable investing?

Environmental

Sustainable investing is about creating a better world by investing in ways that help solve the world's biggest challenges.

Environmenatal aspects include investing in products, services and solutions for the world's biggest challenges, like climate change, water scarcity, pollution and energy use.

Social

Sustainable investing is good for the community.

Social elements refer to actions that support society through community engagement and strong employee relations.

Governance

Sustainable investing is good for companies.

Governance factors consider how effectively companies are led and managed.

Sustainable investing is becoming easier all the time and we are here to help get you started.

Why Invest in Sustainability?

#1 Impact

Sustainable investing is a great way to make a difference with your money.

There is compelling evidence to suggest that sustainable investing can generate good returns, especially in challenging times.

There has never been a better time to align your investments with your values.

#2 Growth

Sustainable investing is focused on long-term value creation.

Investing in positive environmental, social and governance attributes can be a sensible way to secure good return on your investment.

Companies solving global challenges, such as the climate crisis and water scarcity, are well placed to survive and thrive.

#3 Balance

Investing in sustainability can help to manage risk.

Recent research comparing thousands of traditional and sustainable investments over 14-years identified a lower downside risk in sustainable investments.

How to Start Investing Sustainably?

#1 Existing Funds

Existing ESG themed Mutal Funds or Exchange Traded Funds (ETFs) are an easy way to get started, especially if you are an existing investor.

There is a wide selection of funds to choose from, for example, Blackrock has over 300 available through iShares.

If you are an existing investor, adding these to your portfolio will be easy.

If you are new to investing in company shares or funds, you will need your to open a stocks and shares investment account at a company like Hargreaves Lansdown or AJ Bell Youinvest.

#2 Themed Applications

If you are new to investing, it can be difficult to know where to start, which is where ESG themed applications might help.

There are a number of new applications on the market targeting sustainable investment themes.

Examples in the UK market include circa500 and Clim8.

These platforms are designed to be easy to use and take the work out of picking the funds yourself.

They check all the funds for sustainability credentials and performance, so you simply invest your money based on your appetite for risk.

#3 Build your own

You can research individual companies, bonds and funds based on your own sustainablity values.

You can pick established companies, or start-ups or a mixture of both.

You can buy directly into start-ups through platforms such as Crowdcube.

Be warned though, this is the highest risk approach. You are exposed to potentially high returns, but your exposure to loss is also very high.
